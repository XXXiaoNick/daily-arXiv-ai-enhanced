### [1] [Three-Currency HJM for Brazilian Credit Markets](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.29376)
*Raphael Coelho*

Main category: q-fin.MF

TL;DR: 本文构建了一个三币种HJM框架，将公司信用视为独立经济，通过合成通胀和信用汇率连接名义与实体经济。在巴西债券市场发现，同一发行人在不同板块的信用利差存在约640基点的残差，主要可通过零售税收优惠基准解释。

  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 现有定价模型通常假设公司信用在不同债券板块间是一致的，但实际市场可能因投资者结构、税收政策和流动性差异而呈现分割。本文旨在构建一个可检验的理论框架，量化这种分割程度。

Method: 提出三币种HJM框架，将公司信用作为独立经济，通过合成汇率连接三个经济体。推导出可检验的利差恒等式，并利用巴西CDI指数挂钩和IPCA指数挂钩两个债券板块的配对数据（15家发行人，2021-2026年）进行实证检验。

Result: 三年期残差均值为640基点，横截面标准差26基点，在货币紧缩和宽松周期中保持稳定。零售税后无差异基准（基于第12.431号法律）可解释大部分残差，剩余部分与机构参与、契约不对称和流动性差异相关。

Conclusion: 巴西公司债券市场呈现显著分割，税收优惠是主要驱动因素，但机构行为和市场摩擦也起重要作用。框架可用于量化不同市场中信用经济假设的偏差。

Abstract: This paper develops a three-currency Heath-Jarrow-Morton framework in which corporate credit is treated as a separate economy, connected to the nominal and real economies through synthetic inflation and credit exchange rates. The framework produces a testable identity. Under joint no-arbitrage, the credit spread of an issuer expressed over the inflation-rateindexed risk-free curve equals the same issuer's credit spread expressed over the nominalrate-indexed risk-free curve plus the model-implied breakeven inflation forward at the same maturity. The identity holds within any single calibration of the framework. It is empirically falsifiable across two parallel corporate-bond segments of the same market, in a segmented market the two segments may price different corporate credit economies, and the gap between their implied corporate forwards measures the failure of the shared-credit-economy assumption. Applied to Brazilian debenture markets, the framework delivers a sharp empirical finding. Fifteen large issuers placed paper in both the CDI-indexed general-purpose segment and the IPCA-indexed infrastructure segment between January 2021 and February 2026. The within-issuer triangle residual at the 3-year tenor averages 640 basis points, with crosssectional standard deviation of 26 basis points across the 15 issuer means, and remains stable through both the 2021-2023 BCB tightening cycle and the 2024-2026 easing phase. A retail post-tax indifference benchmark anchored on Lei 12.431 closes the bulk of the residual. The remainder is consistent with institutional participation on the CDI side, contractual asymmetries between debentures with different use-of-proceeds restrictions, and segment-specific liquidity gaps.
